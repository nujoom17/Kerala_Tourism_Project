Known for its abundant varieties of coconuts, Kerala is also called the land of coconuts as derived from the Malayalam word "Kera"

Thanks to its long coastline with the Arabian Sea and the tropical climate, Kerala is renowned for its pristine backwaters and sandy beaches, alongside serene rivers and streams that are rejuvented yearly by the local monsoons

Including its topological location next to the majestic Western Ghats, the state boasts a dozen offbeat landscapes for every nature and adventure lover out there

Owing to its historical impact in terms of trade and migration, diversity and multi-culturalism has paved a substantial role in the Malayalee culture. The state boasts a unique blend of folk arts and cultural traditions spanning thousands of years back in the human civilization
